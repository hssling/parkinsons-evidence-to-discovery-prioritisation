# Double-blind Peer-review Audit

## Reviewer 1: Clinical and public-health relevance

**Major concern:** The original version relied heavily on figures and did not give readers a compact numerical summary of prioritised interventions.

**Action taken:** Added Table 1 to the main manuscript, moved the fifth main figure to supplementary material, and kept the NMJI display-item count at five.

**Major concern:** The manuscript could be read as implying clinical use of investigational or repurposed drugs.

**Action taken:** Repeatedly framed pharmacological candidates as requiring confirmatory trials and clarified that prioritisation scores are research and policy metrics, not clinical recommendations.

**Minor concern:** Indian relevance needed to be explicit.

**Action taken:** Preserved India-facing interpretation in the Introduction, Methods and Discussion, including pesticide safety, occupational/rural exposure, primary care, non-communicable disease clinics, head-injury prevention and air quality.

## Reviewer 2: Methods, reproducibility and reporting

**Major concern:** Reference numbers were visually present but not true superscripts in the Word file.

**Action taken:** Added a Word-level superscript formatting pass for in-text citation runs and title-page affiliation markers.

**Major concern:** Supplementary figures were listed but not embedded in the supplementary Word file.

**Action taken:** Rebuilt the supplementary Word file with each supplementary figure embedded below its legend.

**Major concern:** Methods needed clearer outputs and software details.

**Action taken:** Added primary outputs and analysis-environment details, including R 4.5.3, GEOquery, limma, clusterProfiler and ReactomePA.

## Residual risks before upload

- The article remains an evidence-to-discovery study rather than a registered systematic review; this limitation is stated in the Discussion.
- Public dataset and repository availability statements should be checked against the author's preferred disclosure wording before final upload.
- NMJI may request separate high-resolution figure uploads even though the supplementary Word file now embeds figures.
