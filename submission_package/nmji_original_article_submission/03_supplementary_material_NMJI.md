# Supplementary Material

## Supplementary Figure Legends

**Supplementary Figure 1. Final translational priority framework.** Weighted scores classifying candidates into high-priority, promising, and lower-confidence groups.

**Supplementary Figure 2. Pathway validation and actionability matrix.** Pathway modules scored across human evidence, mechanistic plausibility, genetics or omics support, clinical actionability, safety or feasibility, and public-health relevance.

**Supplementary Figure 3. Gene Ontology biological-process enrichment dotplot.** Enrichment of the top-ranked 500 genes from GSE72267.

**Supplementary Figure 4. Exploratory Reactome enrichment dotplot.** Reactome pathway enrichment of the top-ranked 500 genes from GSE72267; results are exploratory because adjusted p values did not remain below 0.05.

**Supplementary Figure 5. Evidence-aligned individual and public-health prevention measures.** Low-risk measures framed as risk-reduction and health-promotion actions rather than guaranteed prevention.

**Supplementary Figure 6. Benchmark target ranking.** Downstream consensus ranking of validation-ready targets integrating pathway evidence, tractability, model relevance, and assayability.

**Supplementary Figure 7. Benchmark evidence matrix.** Heat-map view of evidence and tractability components contributing to downstream target ranking.

**Supplementary Figure 8. Multi-dataset pathway recurrence.** Recurrent pathway signals across GSE72267, GSE7621, and GSE20163.

**Supplementary Figure 9. Benchmark knowledge graph.** Graph representation linking targets, pathways, compounds, model systems, and evidence categories.

## Supplementary Notes

The supplementary figures provide additional detail for readers interested in pathway scoring, exploratory enrichment, prevention framing, target benchmarking, and multi-dataset recurrence. They are intended to support, not replace, the main findings of the manuscript.
